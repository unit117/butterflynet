# GOSIM Paris 2026 - Overview

## The Hackathon
- **Name:** GOSIM Agentic Hackathon 2026
- **Prize Pool:** $10,000 across 5 awards (confirmed on create.gosim.org as of 2026-04-13). Note: earlier web search results claimed €200,000 — this appears inaccurate.
- **Capacity:** ~111 spots total; as of 2026-04-13: 13 teams, 26 registered, 85 spots remaining
- **Registration:** https://create.gosim.org/ — **Free to participate**
- **Eligibility:** In-person only at STATION F. Open to AI engineers, open-source contributors, startup founders, researchers, indie developers, students, and creators.
- **Team Size:** 1–3 people. Can register solo and find teammates during Day 1 team formation.
- **Focus:** Design, develop, and deploy agentic systems that can reason, plan, and act — pushing the boundaries of what open-source AI agents can accomplish in real-world scenarios

### Hackathon Themes (7 Tracks)
1. **Agents Meet Apps** — Reimagine a mainstream app (Twitter, WeChat, TikTok, Spotify, Gmail, etc.) with an agent-first approach.
2. **Claws and Octos** — Ecosystem co-creation around OpenClaw (open-source personal AI assistant) and Octos (OS layer for OpenClaw).
3. **Human–Agent Interaction** — Redefine HCI for the agentic era: intent understanding, context perception, proactive action.
4. **Education** — Reimagine learning with agentic AI.
5. **Content Remixing** — Combine AI capabilities across modalities (text, music, images, video, narration, simulation).
6. **Productivity** — Agents that understand business context, autonomously execute multi-step tasks, coordinate across systems.
7. **Agents with Voices** — Voice-enabled agents combined with smart hardware and IoT devices.

### Official Rules (new, from paris.mofa.ai as of 2026-04-14 — marked "draft, subject to change")

**Section 3 — Eligibility:** Must be legally able to participate in a skills-based competition in local jurisdiction; register with accurate and complete information; agree to all rules and organizer instructions; participate as individual or as part of only one team.

**Section 4 — Team Rules:** Max 3 members per team; a participant may belong to only 1 team; each team submits only 1 project; team membership must be finalized before the submission deadline; multiple submissions by the same team may result in disqualification.

**Section 5 — What You May Build On:** Existing open-source frameworks, models, APIs, SDKs, datasets, and previously developed personal/team code are allowed, provided: the team chooses and builds within a published hackathon theme on create.gosim.org; the team has legal rights to all components; the submission clearly identifies major pre-existing components; and the project's core hackathon value comes from work substantially advanced during the hackathon period.

**Section 6 — Open Source & Repo Requirements:** Each team must maintain a GitHub (or equivalent) project repository accessible to organizer and judges by the submission deadline, with a README explaining the project, setup, and usage, plus a clearly stated OSI-approved open-source license. Acceptable licenses: MIT, Apache-2.0, GPL-3.0, LGPL-3.0, MPL-2.0, or other OSI-approved licenses.

**Section 7 — Submission Requirements (expanded):**
- Required: project name; team name + member info; short description; problem being solved and why it matters; demo link or accessible working artifact; source repo link; demo video (recommended 3–5 minutes); list of technologies used.
- Recommended/optional: architecture diagram; deployment URL; sample workflow or benchmark result.
- Note: "Judges are not required to install, compile, or troubleshoot a project locally. Teams are responsible for making the demo accessible and understandable."

**Section 8 — Demo Requirements:** To be eligible for judging, the project must be demonstrable. A valid demo should make it reasonably easy for judges to verify what the project does, what is actually working, where the agentic behavior appears, what inputs/tools/external systems are involved, and what output/decision/action the system produces. A broken, inaccessible, or non-functional demo at judging time may result in a lower score or disqualification.

**Section 10 — Disqualification Causes:** Plagiarism, unauthorized content use, legal/platform violations, fraudulent uses, non-functional submissions, incomplete materials, offensive/discriminatory content, or interference with judging.

**Section 11 — Code of Conduct:** All participants, organizers, judges, speakers, sponsors, and volunteers must behave professionally and respectfully. No harassment, discrimination, intimidation, doxxing, or disruptive/abusive conduct.

**Section 12 — Intellectual Property:** Participants retain ownership of their project IP (subject to third-party material rights). By participating, each team grants the organizer a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free right to use the team name, project name, screenshots, logos, short descriptions, and demo excerpts for event operations, judging, recap materials, press, community content, and future promotional use related to the hackathon or GOSIM.

**Section 13 — AI, Models, and Third-Party Services:** Proprietary or open-source AI models and third-party APIs may be used unless explicitly restricted by the organizer or sponsor track rules. Teams must disclose material third-party dependencies that are important to understanding how the project works, what is original to the team, and what functionality depends on external services.

**Section 14 — Awards:** The organizer reserves the right to not grant an award in a category if submissions do not meet the expected standard, split awards in the case of a tie or exceptional submissions, and adjust category definitions before final judging if necessary.

### Theme Notes and Example Directions (new, from paris.mofa.ai)
- **Agents with Voices** track explicitly references **EchoKit** — a full-stack open-source voice AI agent toolkit built on Rust with native MCP support, able to connect to any AI model.
- **Agents Meet Apps** — example directions: Agentic X (autonomous posting/curation), Agentic WeChat (intelligent message management), Agentic Spotify (taste-aware playlists).
- **Claws and Octos** — OpenClaw framed as "open-source paradigm for personal AI assistants, self-hosted and user-owned"; Octos as "OS layer for OpenClaw, aimed at family and team agent experiences." Directions: family/team skills, cross-platform portable capabilities, security and permission management with audit, creative skills for games/music/data viz/generative art.
- **Human–Agent Interaction** — directions: beyond-chat paradigms (gestures, spatial, ambient), adaptive interfaces, multi-agent collaborative interfaces, zero-UI agents that run silently and surface only at critical moments.
- **Education** — directions: Socratic AI tutor, Feynman learning partner (students teach the AI), adaptive real-time difficulty systems, immersive history/science exploration.
- **Content Remixing** — directions: cross-modal pipeline from idea to complete film, AI-driven interactive narrative with real-time branching, intelligent presentation generation from oral description, scientific visualization remix console.
- **Productivity** — directions: intelligent business process automation, knowledge management agent, meeting agent (notes/task assignment/tracking), multi-system orchestration across email/calendar/project management/CRM.
