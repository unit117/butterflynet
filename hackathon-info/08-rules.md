# GOSIM Agentic Hackathon 2026 - Official Rules

Saved from user-provided page copy on 2026-04-14.

Status: draft, subject to change.

## 1. Overview

GOSIM Agentic Hackathon is an official hackathon program of GOSIM Paris 2026.

## 2. Event Scope

- The hackathon is part of GOSIM Paris 2026.
- Planned venue: STATION F, Paris.
- The hackathon takes place during the GOSIM Paris 2026 event window.
- Detailed check-in, hacking, submission, demo, and judging timing will be announced separately by the organizer.

## 3. Eligibility

Participants must:

- be legally able to participate in a skills-based competition under the laws of their local jurisdiction
- register using accurate and complete information
- agree to these rules and any additional event instructions published by the organizer
- participate as an individual or as part of only one team

The organizer may refuse or revoke participation if a participant provides false information, violates applicable law, or fails to comply with the rules.

## 4. Team Rules

- Participants may join as an individual or form a team.
- Each team may have up to 3 members.
- A participant may belong to only 1 team.
- Each team may submit only 1 project.
- Team membership must be finalized before the submission deadline unless the organizer explicitly approves a change.
- If multiple submissions are made by the same team, the organizer may decide which submission is valid or disqualify the team.

## 5. What You May Build On

Participants may use existing open-source frameworks, models, APIs, SDKs, datasets, and previously developed personal or team code, provided that:

- the team chooses and builds within a published hackathon theme available on `create.gosim.org`
- the team has the legal right to use the components
- the final submission clearly identifies major pre-existing components
- the project's core hackathon value comes from work substantially advanced during the hackathon period

The organizer and judges evaluate the originality and substance of the work delivered for this event, not merely the existence of an older codebase.

## 6. Open Source and Repository Requirements

Each team must maintain a project repository for its submission. The repository must:

- be hosted on GitHub or an equivalent project repository platform
- be accessible to the organizer and judges by the submission deadline
- include a README explaining the project, setup, and usage
- include a clearly stated open-source license using an OSI-approved license

Examples of acceptable licenses include:

- MIT
- Apache-2.0
- GPL-3.0
- LGPL-3.0
- MPL-2.0
- other OSI-approved licenses

Teams remain responsible for ensuring they have the legal right to publish or share any code, model, dataset, media, or other third-party component included in the repository.

## 7. Submission Requirements

A valid submission should include:

- project name
- team name and team member information
- a short project description
- the problem being solved and why it matters
- a demo link or other accessible working artifact
- source repository link
- a demo video, recommended length 3-5 minutes
- a short list of technologies used

Recommended but not strictly required unless later announced:

- architecture diagram
- deployment URL
- sample workflow or benchmark result

Judges are not required to install, compile, or troubleshoot a project locally. Teams are responsible for making the demo accessible and understandable.

## 8. Demo Requirements

To be eligible for judging, the project must be demonstrable.

A valid demo should make it reasonably easy for judges to verify:

- what the project does
- what part is actually working
- where the agentic behavior appears
- what inputs, tools, or external systems are involved
- what output, decision, or action the system produces

If a demo link is broken, inaccessible, or non-functional at judging time, the team may receive a lower score or be disqualified from award consideration.

## 9. Judging Criteria

Projects will be reviewed using the published judging criteria on `create.gosim.org`.

Each dimension counts for 20% of the total score:

### 9.1 Innovation

Originality and novelty of the idea.

### 9.2 Technical Depth

Architecture, code quality, and technical complexity.

### 9.3 Completeness

Working demo and functional completeness.

### 9.4 Practicality

Real-world problem-solving potential.

### 9.5 Presentation

Clarity and persuasiveness of the demo.

## 10. Disallowed Content and Disqualification

The following may result in score reduction, ineligibility, or disqualification:

- plagiarism or unauthorized use of third-party content, code, data, or media
- projects that violate applicable law or platform policy
- fraudulent, deceptive, abusive, or unsafe use cases
- non-functional submissions with no credible demo
- incomplete submission materials
- offensive, discriminatory, harassing, or hateful content
- attempts to interfere with judging, event systems, or other participants

The organizer may investigate suspected violations and make a final determination at its sole discretion.

## 11. Code of Conduct

All participants, organizers, judges, speakers, sponsors, and volunteers are expected to behave professionally and respectfully.

Participants must not engage in:

- harassment
- discrimination
- intimidation
- doxxing
- disruptive or abusive conduct

If a participant behaves inappropriately, the organizer may take any action it deems appropriate, including warning, removal from the event, or disqualification.

## 12. Intellectual Property

Participants retain ownership of the intellectual property in the projects they create, subject to the rights of any third-party materials they use.

By participating, each team grants the organizer a non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free right to:

- use the team name, project name, screenshots, logos, short descriptions, and demo excerpts
- showcase the project for event operations, judging, recap materials, press, community content, and future promotional use related to the hackathon or GOSIM

Participants are solely responsible for ensuring they have the necessary rights to all code, models, datasets, media, and content included in their submission.

## 13. AI, Models, and Third-Party Services

Participants may use proprietary or open-source AI models and third-party APIs unless explicitly restricted by the organizer or sponsor track rules.

Teams must disclose material third-party dependencies that are important to understanding:

- how the project works
- what is original to the team
- what functionality depends on external services

The organizer may require additional disclosure for finalists.

## 14. Awards

Prize structure, award categories, sponsor prizes, and finalist selection details will be announced separately.

The organizer reserves the right to:

- not grant an award in a category if submissions do not meet the expected standard
- split awards in the event of a tie or exceptional submissions
- adjust category definitions before final judging if necessary

## 15. Organizer Rights

The organizer reserves the right to:

- interpret and apply these rules
- verify participant eligibility and submission validity
- modify scheduling, judging format, or event mechanics when reasonably necessary
- remove any participant or submission that violates these rules or undermines the event
- cancel, suspend, or modify part of the event in the case of technical failure, force majeure, safety concerns, or other circumstances beyond reasonable control

All organizer decisions regarding eligibility, judging process, and awards are final.

## 16. Changes and Clarifications

- The organizer may update these rules, publish FAQs, or release clarifications before the submission deadline.
- If there is any conflict between a summary page, announcement post, and the latest published official rules, the latest published official rules will control.

## 17. Contact

Questions related to registration, rules, submissions, judging, or sponsor tracks should be directed to the official GOSIM hackathon contact channels to be announced by the organizer.
